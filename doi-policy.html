DOI Policy

Digital Object Identifier (DOI) assignment and management policy for VA-RA publications.

Last Updated: February 15, 2026

What is a DOI?

A Digital Object Identifier (DOI) is a unique alphanumeric string assigned to digital objects, such as journal articles, conference papers, and other research outputs. DOIs provide a persistent link to the object's location on the internet, ensuring that your research remains findable and citable even if the URL changes.

Example DOI: 10.5281/va-ra.2026.00142

DOI Assignment

VA-RA assigns DOIs to all published content through our partnership with Crossref, the official DOI registration agency for scholarly publishing.

Content Types That Receive DOIs:

  • Research articles
  • Review articles
  • Conference proceedings
  • Book chapters
  • Editorials and letters
  • Supplementary materials
  • Data sets (when applicable)

DOI Registration Process

DOIs are assigned at the following stages:

  1. Submission: A preliminary DOI is reserved when the manuscript is submitted
  2. Acceptance: The DOI is finalized and registered with Crossref upon acceptance
  3. Publication:The full DOI metadata is published and made searchable in Crossref's database

VA-RA DOI Structure

VA-RA DOIs follow the format:

10.5281/va-ra.[year].[sequence]

  • 10.5281: VA-RA's Crossref prefix
  • va-ra: VA-RA identifier
  • [year]: Year of publication
  • [sequence]: Unique sequential number

DOI Usage Guidelines

For Authors:

  • Include the DOI in all citations of your published work
  • Use the full DOI link format: https://doi.org/[DOI]
  • Add DOIs to metadata when depositing in institutional repositories
  • Include DOIs in reference lists when citing VA-RA publications

For Readers:

  • Click on any DOI to access the published document
  • DOIs can be resolved regardless of URL changes
  • Use DOI look-up services to find documents across publishers

DOI Metadata

All VA-RA DOIs include comprehensive metadata registered with Crossref:

  • Author names and affiliations
  • Article title and abstract
  • Journal name, volume, issue, and page numbers
  • Publication date (online and print)
  • Publisher information
  • License information (Open Access)
  • Funding information
  • Cross-references (citations and references)

DOI Persistence Guarantee

VA-RA guarantees that:

  • All DOIs will remain active indefinitely
  • Content will never be removed from our servers without DOI redirection
  • Retracted articles retain their DOI with "retracted" status
  • Corrected articles receive a new DOI while maintaining link to original
  • DOI resolution is available 24/7
